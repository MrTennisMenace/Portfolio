RSA Decryption


Decryption equation: decrypted text = c^D mod n

    • Note: c = Ciphertext and D = Decrypt Key

Step One: Find the value for n. In order to know n, we need to have the factors p and q. Following from our encryption example we will have our p = 11, q =11, and E = 23.

  • Remember that n = p∗q.
    • n = (11)∗(19) = 209.

Step Two: Find the value for the decryption key D.

  • Perform gcd(E, m); stop at the last non-zero remainder.
    • gcd(23,180):
      • 180 = p ∗ (23) + r
        • Note: 'p' is how many times 23 can go into 180, 'r' means remainder.
      • 180 = 7 ∗ 23 + 19
      • 23 = 1 ∗ 19 + 4
      • 19 = 4 ∗ 4 + 3
      • 4 = 1 ∗ 3 + 1
  • Perform the Extended Eculidean Algorithm:
    • 1 = 4 - 1(3)
      • Make successive subsitutions from the remainders.
        • Substitute 3 with 19 - 4(4).
    • 1 = 4 - 1(19 - 4(4))
      • Distribute.
    • 1 = 4 - 1(19) + 4(4)
      • Combine common values(hint: combine the 4's).
    • 1 = 5(4) - 1(19)
      • Substitute 4 with 23 - 1(19).
    • 1 = 5(23 - 1(19)) - 1(19)
      • Distribute.
    • 1 = 5(23) - 5(19) - 1(19)
      • Combine common values(hint: combine the 19's).
    • 1 = 5(23) - 6(19)
      • Substitute 19 with 180 - 7(23).
    • 1 = 5(23) - 6(180 - 7(23))
      • Distribute.
    • 1 = 5(23) - 6(180) + 42(23)
      • Combine common values(hint: combine the 23's).
    • 1 = 47(23) - 6(180)
      • Our Decryption Key is 47.
      • Notice 23 is our Encryption Key and 180 is our m(totient of n).
      • When you do the Extended Euclidean Algorithm and you are left with your E and m, the value that is multiplied with E is your D.

Step Three: For each individual cipher text c, perform the equation: c^D mod n. Make sure to only decrypt a letter at a time.

  • Decrypt the cipher text: 122, 0, 171, 68.
    • 122 ^ 47 mod 209 = 12
    • 0 ^ 47 mod 209 = 0
    • 171 ^ 47 mod 209 = 19
    • 68 ^ 47 mod 209 = 7
  • 12, 0, 19, 7 translates to: MATH
